Midjourney Beginners Guide 2026: How to Create Stunning AI Art

📅 April 3, 2026⏱ 11 min read✍️ AI Tools Hub Editorial

Midjourney is widely considered the best AI image generator in 2026, producing images of a quality that regularly amazes even professional designers. But for beginners, the Discord-based interface and prompt-writing can be confusing. This complete guide will take you from zero to creating stunning AI artwork.

What is Midjourney?

Midjourney is an AI image generation tool that creates images from text descriptions called prompts. You describe what you want to see, and Midjourney's AI model interprets your description and generates four image options. Unlike most AI tools that have a website, Midjourney operates through Discord — a messaging app originally designed for gaming communities.

It has quickly become the go-to tool for professional designers, concept artists, marketers, and content creators because of its consistently exceptional artistic quality. Starting at $10 per month, it is also more affordable than hiring a designer for every visual task.

Step 1: Getting Started with Midjourney

To start using Midjourney, you need a Discord account (free) and a Midjourney subscription. Here is the setup process:

  1. Go to discord.com and create a free account if you do not have one.
  2. Go to midjourney.com and click "Sign In" — this connects your Discord account.
  3. Choose a subscription plan (Basic at $10/month is a good starting point).
  4. You will be taken to the Midjourney Discord server, or you can add the Midjourney bot to your own server or use it in Direct Messages.

Many users prefer creating a private Discord server and adding the Midjourney bot there, so your images are not mixed in with thousands of other users' work in public channels.

Step 2: Your First Prompt

In any channel where the Midjourney bot is active, type /imagine followed by your description. For example:

/imagine a golden retriever puppy sitting in a field of sunflowers, soft morning light, photography style

Within about 60 seconds, Midjourney will generate four image variations. You can then upscale any of them (U1, U2, U3, U4) to get a larger, more detailed version, or generate variations (V1, V2, V3, V4) to explore different interpretations of that image.

Beginner Tip: Your first few prompts will probably not look exactly how you imagined. That is completely normal. Midjourney is a tool you learn through experimentation. Generate lots of images, see what works, and gradually refine your prompting approach.

Step 3: Writing Good Prompts

The quality of your prompts directly determines the quality of your images. Here are the key elements of an effective Midjourney prompt:

Subject

Start with what you want in the image. Be specific. Instead of "a woman," try "a middle-aged Indian woman with dark hair, wearing a saree, standing in a marketplace."

Style and Medium

Specify the artistic style you want. Examples: oil painting, watercolour illustration, photorealistic photography, anime style, cinematic film still, concept art, digital art, sketch, charcoal drawing.

Lighting

Lighting dramatically changes the mood of an image. Try: golden hour lighting, dramatic studio lighting, soft natural light, neon glow, candlelight, overcast daylight, backlit.

Mood and Atmosphere

Words like ethereal, melancholic, vibrant, mysterious, serene, dramatic, peaceful help set the emotional tone of the image.

Camera and Lens Details (for photography style)

If you want photorealistic images: shot on Canon 5D Mark IV, 85mm lens, shallow depth of field, bokeh background, macro photography, aerial drone shot.

Step 4: Key Midjourney Commands

Understanding the main parameters helps you get more control over your results:

  • --ar 16:9 — Sets the aspect ratio. Use 16:9 for widescreen, 1:1 for square, 9:16 for vertical/mobile.
  • --style raw — Reduces Midjourney's default artistic enhancement for more photorealistic results.
  • --chaos 0-100 — Higher values produce more varied, unexpected results. Lower values produce more predictable outcomes.
  • --no [element] — Tells Midjourney to exclude something. For example: --no text, watermarks, blurry
  • --stylize 0-1000 — Controls how strongly Midjourney applies its artistic interpretation. Lower values are more literal.

Step 5: Prompt Examples That Work

Product Photography

A luxury skincare serum bottle on a marble surface, surrounded by fresh roses and green leaves, professional product photography, soft diffused lighting, white background, high detail --ar 1:1

Business Illustration

A modern office with diverse professionals collaborating around a large screen showing data visualisations, flat design illustration style, teal and navy colour palette, clean geometric shapes --ar 16:9

Portrait Photography

Portrait of a confident businesswoman, natural window light, shallow depth of field, Canon R5, 85mm f/1.4, professional headshot style --ar 2:3 --style raw

Fantasy Art

A floating island with waterfalls cascading into clouds below, ancient temple ruins overgrown with jungle vines, golden sunset light, dramatic cinematic composition, digital art style --ar 16:9

Midjourney Pricing in 2026

Midjourney has four plans. The Basic plan at $10/month gives you 200 image generations per month — enough for casual use. The Standard plan at $30/month gives you unlimited relaxed generations (slower) and 15 hours of fast generation. Most regular users find the Standard plan is the sweet spot between price and usage.

Tips for Getting the Most from Midjourney

  • Save prompts that work well in a notes file — you will reuse them often.
  • Use the Midjourney web interface at midjourney.com/imagine for a more user-friendly experience.
  • Look at what other users are creating in public galleries for inspiration and prompt ideas.
  • Use /describe to upload an image you like and Midjourney will suggest prompts that might recreate its style.
  • Combine multiple styles: "in the style of Studio Ghibli meets National Geographic photography" produces interesting hybrid results.
